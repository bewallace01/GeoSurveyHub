## KEYWORD PLACEMENT GUIDE (how to use these in the site)

### Page titles and H1 headings
Use the primary keyword naturally in the title and H1.
"How to Start a Land Surveying Company — Licenses, Costs & Revenue"
"LiDAR vs Photogrammetry: Which Should You Choose?"
"What is RTK GPS? A Plain-English Guide for Surveyors"

### Meta descriptions
Include primary keyword + supporting keyword in first sentence.
"Learn how to start a land surveying company from licensing requirements
to startup costs and revenue potential. A complete guide for aspiring
surveying entrepreneurs."

### FAQ sections (highest SEO leverage)
Each FAQ uses a Tier 2 long-tail keyword as the question.
"How long does it take to become a licensed surveyor?"
"What does a land survey cost per acre?"
Answer directly in 2–3 sentences with specific figures.

### Body copy
First paragraph: primary keyword appears naturally.
H2 headings: supporting keywords as phrases.
Glossary terms: Tier 2 keywords as defined terms.

### Internal anchor text
Use descriptive, keyword-rich anchor text for internal links:
"Learn more about drone LiDAR accuracy" (links to lidar.html)
"Take the equipment quiz to find your setup" (links to guide.html)
"Explore mobile mapping systems" (links to trucks.html)

Existing competition in the educational surveying content space:
- Equipment manufacturers (Trimble, Leica, DJI): rank for own brand, not education
- NSPS and NCEES: license info only, no educational content or guides
- Geo Week News: industry news but no beginner guides
- Academic programs: rank for degree programs, not practical startup content
- YouTube channels: video content, no structured long-form written content
- Reddit (r/surveying): community Q&A, not structured content

The gap: NOBODY has built a structured, well-designed, educational hub that
explains geospatial technology AND helps people start companies AND stays
current with industry news. GeoSurveyHub is positioned to own this space.

Key advantage: GeoSurveyHub's MNTN aesthetic makes the content feel credible
and professional — most surveying content looks like it was built in 2008.
Design signals trust, and trust drives rankings and time-on-page.

## CONTENT QUALITY SIGNALS (for AI search / GEO optimization)

To rank in AI-powered search (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini), content must:

1. Answer the question in the FIRST sentence of each section
2. Include specific, verifiable figures (prices, accuracy specs, timelines)
3. Reference authoritative organizations (NCEES, NSPS, ASPRS, ACSM)
4. Use the exact technical vocabulary the industry uses (don't simplify away precision)
5. Be structured with clear H2/H3 headings that mirror search queries
6. Include FAQ sections with schema markup
7. Link to primary sources (NCEES, manufacturer spec sheets)
8. Stay current: include 2025/2026 dates and recent product references

Content that does all 8 of these will appear directly in AI search answers.
Content that does none of them will not rank regardless of SEO optimization.
