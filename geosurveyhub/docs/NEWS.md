**Date:** February 22, 2026
**Category:** LiDAR

## Article 2
**Title:** RIEGL Debuts "Ultimate LiDAR" VUX-820-G — Topo + Bathymetric in One Drone Payload
**Date:** October 2025 (shown at INTERGEO 2025)
**Category:** LiDAR, Drones
**Source:** Inside Unmanned Systems
**Summary:**
At INTERGEO 2025, RIEGL introduced the VUX-820-G, a 5.7kg all-in-one drone LiDAR sensor
that simultaneously captures topographic terrain AND shallow-water bathymetric data
(to roughly twice Secchi depth). It features an embedded RiLOC-F-inside GNSS/IMU module
for direct georeferencing — eliminating external navigation boxes. Positional accuracy:
2–3cm after post-processing. Also announced: RiLOC-F-inside is now standard across the
entire VUX-series (VUX-100-25, -120-23, -160-23, -180-24).
**Why it matters:**
For coastal surveys, riverine mapping, or any project that crosses land and water,
previously you needed two separate surveys. The VUX-820-G does both in one flight.
**Tags:** LiDAR, RIEGL, Drone, Bathymetric, New Product

## Article 8
**Title:** Gaussian Splatting Goes Mainstream in GIS — ArcGIS Native Support Arrives
**Date:** January 2026
**Category:** Software, Technology Trends
**Source:** Mosaic / Intergeo 2025 recap
**Summary:**
Gaussian Splatting — a technique that creates photorealistic, navigable 3D scenes from
images — is now natively supported in major GIS tools including ArcGIS. It bridges the
gap between the precision of point clouds and the visual expressiveness of photography.
File sizes are shrinking, rendering speeds are improving, and adoption among geospatial
professionals is accelerating rapidly heading into 2026.
**Why it matters:**
Digital twins and as-built deliverables are getting dramatically easier to share with
clients and project stakeholders who don't have specialized GIS software.
**Tags:** Software, GIS, Gaussian Splatting, 3D, Visualization, Trend

## Article 11
**Title:** DJI Zenmuse L2 vs L3 — What's Actually Different in the Field
**Date:** Ongoing / Evergreen
**Category:** Drones, LiDAR
**Source:** GeoSurveyHub Editorial
**Summary:**
The DJI Zenmuse L2 (launched 2023) delivers 240,000 points/sec, 450m detection range,
and an integrated 4/3 CMOS camera for true-color point clouds. It runs on the Matrice
350 RTK platform. The L1 (previous gen) remains compatible with both M300 RTK and
M350 RTK and offers a lower price point for teams where the L2's extended range or
higher point density isn't required. Key question: if your projects are under 120m AGL
in open terrain, the L1 remains cost-competitive.
**Tags:** DJI, LiDAR, Drone, Comparison, Zenmuse
