Survey Drones — Your Eye in the Sky

The aircraft is the platform; the sensor does the measurement. Multirotors carry LiDAR and photogrammetry payloads for topo, construction, and inspection. Fixed-wing systems cover more acres per day when terrain allows.

Who needs this?

Teams mapping 10 acres to thousands of acres — topographic surveys, progress monitoring, stockpiles, and corridor segments where crewed aircraft would be overkill.

What to budget for

  • Airframe + RTK: Positioning for the drone itself — often paired with PPK/RTK for ground control reduction.
  • Payload: RGB for photogrammetry, multispectral for ag, LiDAR for canopy and engineering-grade surfaces.
  • Software: Flight planning, processing (Pix4D, Agisoft, DJI Terra, etc.), and GIS export.
  • Compliance: FAA Part 107 in the U.S. for commercial work.

LiDAR or camera?

Open terrain with good lighting → photogrammetry often wins on cost. Forested sites, weak texture, or night-adjacent constraints → LiDAR payloads. Many firms run both over time.
