Foundation

The 5 Technologies Every Surveyor Should Know

These are the pillars of professional measurement — from lasers to satellites to vehicle-mounted systems. Click through to category pages for gear and specs.

01

LiDAR — the laser that sees through trees

Pulsed lasers build dense 3D point clouds. Multi-return sensors can map ground beneath canopy. Best for: forestry DTM, corridors, as-built. Not ideal for: texture-only deliverables without a separate camera.

LiDAR systems
02

Photogrammetry — photos become measurements

Overlapping images reconstruct 3D geometry via Structure from Motion. Best for: open sites, orthophotos, colorized meshes. Not ideal for: dense canopy without ground shots.

Photogrammetry guide
03

GNSS / RTK — GPS that thinks in centimeters

Real-time or post-processed corrections yield survey-grade positions for rovers and drones. Best for: boundaries, stakeout, control networks. Not ideal for: long periods under heavy multipath without a good sky view.

GNSS & RTK
04

Total stations — the original precision instrument

Angles plus EDM distances to prism or reflectorless targets — still the gold standard for many legal and engineering surveys. Best for: control, construction layout, deed work.

Total stations
05

Mobile mapping — surveying at driving speed

LiDAR + cameras + GNSS/IMU on a vehicle captures corridors and cities fast. Best for: roads, rails, urban digital twins. Accuracy: often 2–5 cm in open sky; degrades in deep urban canyons.

Specs

What the Numbers Actually Mean

Manufacturers advertise pulse rates, ranges, and accuracies. Here's how to read them without getting lost in the brochure.

PRR / pulse rate
Pulses per second — higher often means denser data at a given speed and altitude.
Range
Maximum distance to a reflective surface; real-world performance varies with material and angle.
Point density
Points per square meter — depends on altitude, speed, and sensor, not one number in isolation.
IMU grade
Motion sensing quality; better IMUs maintain pose when GNSS is briefly lost.
RTK accuracy
Typical high-end specs cite ~8 mm + 1 ppm horizontal — verify in your environment.

Workflow

From Field to Deliverable

1

Capture

Drone mission apps, total station jobs, or mobile mapping runs — raw observations plus calibration.

2

Process & classify

LiDAR: ground separation, noise removal. Photogrammetry: SfM and dense matching. AI classification is now production-grade with human QA.

3

GIS & CAD

ArcGIS Pro, QGIS, Civil 3D — contours, surfaces, vectors for client standards.

4

Deliver

LAS/LAZ, COG, GeoPDF, plus Gaussian Splatting viewers where visuals matter more than raw vectors.

FAQ

Common questions

When should I use LiDAR instead of photogrammetry?
Choose LiDAR for canopy penetration, night-capable capture, and consistent geometric accuracy without relying on texture. Choose photogrammetry when you need color-rich meshes and orthophotos in open terrain at lower hardware cost.
What is RTK GPS in surveying?
RTK applies correction data from a base or VRS network so your rover or drone achieves centimeter accuracy in real time — essential for legal surveys, machine control, and reducing GCP count.
How accurate is drone LiDAR?
With good flight planning and PPK/RTK, vertical accuracy in the 2–5 cm class is common for professional payloads. Forested terrain and GNSS outages increase residuals — always budget for QA.
