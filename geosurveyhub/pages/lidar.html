LiDAR — The Laser That Builds the World

LiDAR fires laser pulses and measures time-of-flight to build a 3D point cloud. Combined with GNSS and IMU data, it produces survey-grade terrain and objects — even under vegetation when multi-return sensors are used.

Who needs this?

Forestry and environmental teams, engineering firms doing topo in tree cover, corridor mappers, as-built documentarians, and anyone who needs a consistent surface model when photogrammetry alone cannot see the ground.

Types of LiDAR

  • Terrestrial (TLS): Tripod-mounted, millimeter-level at close range — BIM, as-built, structural.
  • Mobile (MLS): Vehicle or backpack — urban and corridor at driving or walking speed.
  • Airborne / drone (ALS): Covers large areas; drone systems bring professional capture to smaller teams.
  • SLAM: Maps without GPS — mines, tunnels, indoor complexes.
