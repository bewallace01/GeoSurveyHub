Mobile Mapping & SLAM

Mobile systems capture LiDAR and imagery while moving—ideal for highways, rails, utilities, and city-scale digital twins. SLAM extends similar ideas where satellite positioning is absent: mines, buildings, and complex indoor spaces.

Vehicle-mounted (MLS)

Roads and rails at driving speed. GNSS/IMU couples with laser scanners; control from checkpoints limits drift. Compare with mobile mapping trucks product context and LiDAR fundamentals.

Backpack & handheld

Pedestrian speed for campuses, trails, and facilities—often SLAM-forward with GNSS when available. Great for areas a truck cannot reach.

When to choose static TLS

When you need maximum precision on discrete facades or tight volumes, tripod terrestrial LiDAR may beat mobile capture—budget time for registration.

Processing

Trajectory adjustment and classification feed into point cloud processing and AI-assisted extraction.
